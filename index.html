Overview

Player Retention

Player retention refers to a game's ability to keep its user base engaged over time. In the competitive gaming market, understanding why players stay or leave is critical for studios and publishers looking to maximize lifetime value and community growth.

This project uses Steam's large-scale behavioral dataset to identify the key signals of churn — and to build models that can proactively surface competing games and personalized recommendations to re-engage at-risk players before they leave.

The Process

Data Gathering

Collecting Steam behavioral logs, game metadata, user reviews, and playtime data across millions of player sessions and thousands of titles.

Reporting

Communicating findings through interactive dashboards and visual reports that surface churn risk scores and recommendation outputs.

Analysis

Applying EDA, feature engineering, and statistical methods to identify patterns in player behavior that precede disengagement.

Strategic Advising

Translating model outputs into actionable retention strategies — from personalized notifications to competing game recommendations.

Analysis

Techniques

Descriptive

Summarizes historical Steam behavior to characterize baseline engagement, session frequency, and playtime distribution across the player base.

Predictive

Uses gradient boosting and neural network models to forecast churn probability based on early behavioral signals detected within the first 7 days.

Diagnostic

Investigates root causes of player drop-off by drilling into cohort-level patterns, genre preferences, and social graph interactions.

Prescriptive

Drives personalized game recommendations and re-engagement triggers using collaborative filtering on sparse interaction matrices.

Challenges

Steam data is sparse and noisy — many players have minimal interaction histories, making it difficult to build reliable behavioral profiles from limited signals.

Class imbalance between churned and retained players requires careful handling through oversampling, weighted loss functions, and threshold tuning to avoid biased predictions.

Solutions

We apply feature imputation, temporal aggregation, and cold-start strategies to extract meaningful signals even from sparse user profiles.

Ensemble methods with calibrated probabilities and SMOTE-based resampling address class imbalance while preserving model generalizability across diverse player segments.
